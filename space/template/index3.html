GRAVITATIONAL WAVE

Gravitational waves are disturbances in the curvature of spacetime,

generated by accelerated masses,

that propagate as waves outward from their source at the speed of light

Key Facts

General Theory OF Relativity

They Prove General Theory Of Relativity

Gravitational radiation

Gravitational waves transport energy as gravitational radiation, a form of radiant energy similar to electromagnetic radiation.

Sources

Black hole binaries, Supernovae, Spinning neutron stars, Inflation

Current Projects

Large-scale physics experiment and observatory to detect cosmic gravitational waves as an astronomical tool.
LIGO
Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory
Large interferometer designed to detect gravitational waves predicted by the general theory of relativity.
VIRGO
Virgo interferometer
Mission led by the European Space Agency to detect and accurately measure gravitational waves—tiny ripples in the fabric of space-time—from astronomical sources.
LISA
Laser Interferometer Space Antenna
